Comet Anatomy

Nucleus

The nucleus of a comet is its solid core, made of ice, dust, and small rocky particles. It is usually only a few kilometers across.

Coma

The coma is the cloud of gas and dust that forms around the nucleus when a comet approaches the Sun. The heat causes sublimation of the cometâ€™s ices.

Ion Tail

The ion tail is made of charged particles that are swept away from the comet by the solar wind. It is always directed away from the Sun.

Dust Tail

The dust tail is composed of small solid particles that are pushed away from the comet by the pressure of sunlight. This tail typically curves behind the comet.
